\section{System Design and Methodology}

\subsection{Overall RAG Pipeline}
The system is designed as a citation-grounded Retrieval-Augmented Generation pipeline for Vietnamese labor-law question answering. Given a user question, the assistant retrieves relevant legal evidence from a scoped corpus, assembles the selected evidence, generates an answer, and checks whether the cited legal bases are supported by the retrieved context.

The pipeline is divided into two main stages. The first stage is the offline legal knowledge build. In this stage, selected legal documents are cleaned, structured, enriched with metadata, and indexed. The output of this stage is stored in two complementary forms: a Qdrant hybrid index for text retrieval~\cite{qdrantdocs} and a Neo4j legal graph for legal relationships.

The second stage is the online answering process. When a user submits a question, the system retrieves candidate provisions from Qdrant, expands related evidence through Neo4j when needed, merges and filters the results, and prepares a bounded context for answer generation. The generated answer is then validated before being returned to the user.

\subsection{Scoped Legal Corpus}
The corpus is intentionally limited to a selected set of Vietnamese labor-law documents. This bounded scope makes the system easier to evaluate and also defines when the assistant should refuse a question or mark it as insufficient-context.

The final corpus contains six document groups: the Labor Code 2019 and the labor-related subset of the Civil Procedure Code 2015, Decree 135/2020/ND-CP and Decree 145/2020/ND-CP, and Circular 09/2020/TT-BLDTBXH and Circular 10/2020/TT-BLDTBXH. These documents were selected because they cover common labor-law issues such as employment contract termination, retirement age, minor workers, working time, and labor dispute procedures.

Vietnamese legal documents are often connected to each other through hierarchy, implementation guidance, and legal references. A code may define the general rule, a decree may provide implementation details, and a circular may contain forms, lists, or technical guidance. Some documents may also clarify, overlap with, or replace earlier rules. Therefore, the corpus cannot be treated as a flat collection of unrelated passages.

\subsection{Hierarchy-Aware Legal Processing}
The corpus pipeline does not split legal documents into arbitrary text windows only. Instead, it preserves legal units such as documents, articles, clauses, points, and appendices, following the hierarchical structure of Vietnamese legal documents. Each unit is converted into an evidence chunk with its own citation information and metadata.

This design is important because legal meaning depends on structure. A clause belongs to an article, an article belongs to a document, and a lower-level document may provide guidance for a higher-level rule. If this structure is lost, the retriever may return a passage that is textually similar but legally incomplete.

Each evidence chunk contains retrieval text and citation metadata. The retrieval text is used to search for relevant provisions. The metadata records information such as document name, article number, clause, point, appendix, legal rank, topic, actor, and issue type. This allows later components to connect retrieved text with its legal source.

The result of this step is a structured corpus that can support both semantic search and citation validation.

\subsection{Hybrid Retrieval Pipeline}
The first retrieval layer is implemented with Qdrant. The system uses hybrid retrieval, combining dense semantic search and sparse lexical search.

Dense retrieval is useful when the user asks a question in natural language. It can find provisions that are related in meaning, even if the wording is different from the legal document. This is important because real users rarely use exact legal terms.

Sparse retrieval is useful for exact legal references. Legal questions may contain article numbers, document names, legal terms, or phrases that should be matched directly. These details can be missed by semantic search alone.

The system combines these signals to produce an initial set of candidate legal provisions. It also applies reference recovery when a user explicitly mentions an article, clause, point, or document. This helps the retriever avoid missing legal coordinates that are important for the answer.

\subsection{Policy-Controlled Legal Graph Expansion}
After the initial retrieval step, the system can expand the evidence using the Neo4j legal graph. This design follows the general idea of graph-augmented retrieval, where graph structure helps connect related information units for retrieval support. The graph represents legal relationships between documents, articles, clauses, points, appendices, topics, and related provisions.

This component is needed because legal relevance is not always the same as textual similarity. A retrieved provision may refer to another article. A decree may explain how a Labor Code rule should be applied. A circular may contain the required form or technical detail. In these cases, the answer may require legal provisions that are connected by structure or reference, even if they do not closely match the user's wording.

Graph expansion is controlled by policy rules. The system does not simply add every neighboring node in the graph. Instead, it considers the query type and the kind of legal relationship before adding graph-based evidence. This is important because related legal provisions may still be distracting or unnecessary for a specific question.

Neo4j is therefore used to support legal evidence assembly, not to replace text retrieval. Qdrant finds the initial relevant provisions, while Neo4j helps recover legally connected evidence when the question requires more than one source.

\subsection{Context Budgeting}
The evidence retrieved from Qdrant and Neo4j is merged, deduplicated, filtered, and reranked before answer generation~\cite{cormack2009rrf}. This step prepares the final context that will be passed to the answer generation module.

Context budgeting is necessary because the system cannot send unlimited legal text to the generator. Too much context can make the answer less focused and may introduce related but incorrect provisions. At the same time, too little context may cause the system to miss an important legal basis.

The system therefore selects a bounded number of contexts. When needed, it may include parent articles, related clauses, or appendix-level information to make the evidence more complete. The goal is to provide enough legal support for the answer while avoiding unnecessary legal text.

\subsection{Citation Validation and Refusal}
The answer layer is designed to generate responses only from selected legal contexts. In the deployed system, a configured language-model provider can generate grounded answers from the retrieved evidence. For reproducible evaluation, the final benchmark uses a deterministic extractive generation mode to avoid variability from external model providers.

After an answer is generated, the citation validator checks whether the cited legal bases are present in the retrieved evidence. It also checks whether the answer uses unsupported article numbers or cites provisions that were not part of the selected context. This step reduces the risk of unsupported legal citations in generated text.

The system also includes insufficient-context behavior. If a question requires a legal document outside the indexed corpus, or if the retrieved evidence is not enough to support an answer, the assistant should refuse or mark the response as insufficient-context. This is important because the system is limited to a scoped corpus and should not answer as if it covers all Vietnamese law.
