\frontmattersection{ABSTRACT}
This thesis presents an AI assistant for Vietnamese labor-law questions, with a case-study focus on employment contract termination and related employment issues. The system is designed as a citation-grounded legal information assistant over a scoped corpus that includes selected labor-law documents, decrees, circulars, and labor-related civil procedure provisions. To improve legal grounding, the project preserves legal structure during corpus processing, represents relevant provisions in Qdrant and Neo4j, retrieves supporting evidence, generates answers from that evidence, and validates whether the final citations are present in the retrieved context. The implemented assistant uses a FastAPI backend, a React/Vite frontend, Supabase, Qdrant, Neo4j, and a configured language-model provider. The system is evaluated on a fixed benchmark of 100 questions, including both in-corpus and out-of-corpus cases, to measure retrieval performance, answer quality, citation validation, and refusal behavior. The results show that graph-augmented retrieval and citation validation can strengthen legal evidence control, while retrieval completeness remains the main limitation. The assistant is intended to support access to legal information within the indexed corpus and does not replace professional legal advice.

\textbf{Keywords}: Vietnamese labor law; Legal question answering; Retrieval-Augmented Generation; Hybrid retrieval; Legal graph; Citation validation.
