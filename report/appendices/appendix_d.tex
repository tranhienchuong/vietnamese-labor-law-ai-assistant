\section{Additional Evaluation Examples}
\label{appendix:evaluation-examples}

Table~\ref{tab:appendix-evaluation-cases} summarizes representative cases from
the final 100-query evaluation. The examples are selected to show both
successful behavior and the main remaining retrieval weaknesses.

\begin{table}[H]
  \centering
  \small
  \caption{Representative final evaluation cases.}
  \label{tab:appendix-evaluation-cases}
  \begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}L{0.17\textwidth}L{0.24\textwidth}L{0.34\textwidth}>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
    \toprule
    Case Type & Tested Capability & Outcome & Interpretation \\
    \midrule
    Simple pass & Employee notice period for unilateral termination & Pass. The system retrieved and cited Labor Code Article 35. & Direct lookup works when the question closely matches the legal issue. \\
    Multi-source pass & Female retirement age in 2026 & Pass. The system combined the Labor Code, Decree 135, and the retirement-age table. & Graph/table expansion helps when the answer requires connected legal sources. \\
    Correct refusal & Regional minimum wage in 2026 & Pass refusal. The system returned insufficient context because the wage instrument was outside the corpus. & Scope control prevents unsupported legal claims. \\
    Paraphrase failure & Temporary transfer described in informal wording & Fail. The system missed the required Labor Code Article 29 evidence. & Informal user wording remains difficult for retrieval. \\
    Appendix/table failure & Domestic-worker contract form in Decree 145 & Fail. The system missed the required form/appendix evidence. & Forms and appendices need stronger table-aware retrieval. \\
    Hard-negative failure & Holiday overtime pay confused with overtime limits & Fail. Retrieval mixed pay rules with hour-limit provisions. & Legal relatedness is not always answer relevance. \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabularx}
\end{table}
